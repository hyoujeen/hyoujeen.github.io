Hi, I am You Jeen Ha
(하유진), focusing on
tech ethics & policy.

How can we democratize tech/AI ethics in the form of an ongoing whole-of-society discourse?

YOU JEEN (유진)'s interests lie in tech ethics & policy with a focus on collaborative deliberation in governance. She was a Project Coordinator for Research with Harvard Law School's Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society, the founder of podcast series Saram in the World, and a consultant with Kigumi Group. During her Fulbright fellowship in South Korea, she researched the Moon administration’s multi-stakeholder policymaking process for 4IR and AI ethics at KAIST.
She holds a B.A. in Computer Science and Philosophy from Smith College, where she completed an honors thesis on causality and machine learning, evaluating how current statistical practices are used to implement explainable AI (XAI). Over the years, she was accepted to various conferences, including the international ACM FAccT Conference (see her paper and her presentation!). She studied abroad at the University of Oxford in Spring 2019, centering her studies on theoretical AI and philosophy of cognitive science.
Born in Seoul but raised in the U.S., You Jeen is proud of her Korean-American identity. With this identity serving as the foundation, she seeks to further cross-national collaboration in tech ethics and policy. In her spare time, she enjoys watching movies, decompressing through PC gaming, digital painting, tactical combat, and spending time with her family and chihuahua.

Passion Projects

After completing her Fulbright fellowship in Korea, You Jeen decided to work on making a think space for cross-national discussions surrounding tech ethics and policy. She envisions this platform to be a kind of Habermasian public sphere, calling for the involvement and participation of all relevant stakeholders. This think space centers on amplifying the voices of civil society, especially those of the underrepresented and the MZ Generation, many of whom have walked through life as 'digital natives.'

On another note, she believes that engaging with the creative arts calms the soul while also stimulating originality and open-minded thinking. By working to understand how different elements and forms interact with one another to shape a cohesive whole, she tries to appreciate the complexities of the world we live in, including its strengths and flaws.
